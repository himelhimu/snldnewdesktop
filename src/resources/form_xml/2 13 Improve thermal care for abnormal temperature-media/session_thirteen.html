If a baby’s temperature is slightly below normal (35.50-36.50C /95.90 -97.70F), make sure that the room is warm (at least 25-280C) and without drafts. Replace wet clothing and ensure that the baby is dry. Maintain skin-to-skin care during this time. If skin-to-skin care is not being used, ensure that the baby is in dry clothing and properly wrapped with a head covering and socks in place.

Cover mother and baby with additional blanket. Check the baby’s temperature every hour until it is normal. Continue breastfeeding during re-warming.

Using these steps, almost all babies with a low temperature will have a normal temperature.

But if the low temperature does not rise after one hour of attempts of warming or if the temperature is <35.50C/ 95.90F, this is a Danger Sign. For these babies, provide adequate care urgently and continue skin-to-skin contact during this time.

A temperature that is > 37.50C/ 99.50F may also be dangerous. Wrapping a baby in too many clothes, leaving a baby in direct sunlight, or placing a baby too close to a fire or heater can cause a high temperature. Improper use of incubators and radiant warmers may cause high temperature. A high temperature may also be a sign of infection.

A temperature > 37.50C/ 99.50F that is not caused by exposure to too much heat and does not become normal when the heat source is removed is a danger sign.
