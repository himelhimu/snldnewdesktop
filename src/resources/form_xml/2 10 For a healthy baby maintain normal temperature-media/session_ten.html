A baby begins to lose heat even before the body temperature falls. For this reason, preventing heat loss should begin with skin-to-skin care at birth. Skin-to-skin care should continue for at least two hours. If illness of mother prevents providing skin-to-skin care, another family member can provide skin-to-skin care.

Other ways to prevent heat loss are to keep the room warm, and eliminate drafts and contact with wet or cold surfaces. Do not bathe a baby prior to 72 hours after birth, or longer if the baby is low birth weight or small.

After skin-to-skin care, wrap the baby in a clean, dry blanket or cloth. Wrap securely, but not so tightly that breathing is difficult. Babies may also be dressed in dry cloths, or a diaper/nappy and shirt. The head should be covered. The amount of clothing should be appropriate for the temperature around the baby. This usually means 1-2 more layers of clothes than are required for adults to be comfortable. Babies may become too hot if placed in direct sunlight, or if placed too close to heaters or stoves.
