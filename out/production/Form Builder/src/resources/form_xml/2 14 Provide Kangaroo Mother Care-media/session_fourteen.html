Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC) is a standard, protocol-based care for preterm (born before 37 weeks of gestation) and/or Low birth weight (LBW-billow 2000 gm)newborn based on skin-to-skin contact between the newborn and the mother or the care-giver. It promotes effective thermal control, breastfeeding, infection prevention and bonding. KMC can provide better survival of pre-term babies compared to standard incubator care. KMC should be initiated at hospital and continued at home. Mother and other family members (father, grandparents, and aunt) should be properly counseled to provide KMC. KMC is affordable.

Components of KMC:

  • Skin-to-skin (STS) contact
  • Exclusive breastfeeding
  • Early discharge and regular follow-up

Benefits of KMC:

Baby-

  • Good thermal control and better weight gain
  • Increased breast feeding rate and duration
  • Better infection prevention
  • Less morbidity and early discharge

Mother-

  • Stronger bonding between mother and baby
  • Increased successful breastfeeding rates
  • Increased self-confidence and satisfaction
  • Reduced hospital stay

Eligibility criteria for KMC:

Baby-

  • All stable pre-term/LBW babies with more than2000 gm birth weight- KMC can be initiated soon after birth
  • Birth weight within 1200 to 1800 gm- may need some special care initially; KMC needs to be initiated after the baby becomes stable
  • Birth weight <1200 gm- KMC can be initiated after initial stabilization by advanced care for a few days to few weeks after birth

Mother-

  • Willingness to provide KMC after counseling on KMC
  • Full time availability (mother or any other family member) to provide care. Other family members can offer intermittent STS care to support the mother

KMC position:

  • Baby should be placed between the mothers’ breast in an upright position
  • Head should be turned to one side and in a slightly extended position
  • Hip should be flexed and abducted in a frog like position and arms should also be flexed
  • Support the baby’s bottom with a sling or binder
  • lKMC can be provided using any front open dress

Monitoring during KMC:

  • Temperature
  • Air way, breathing, heart rate
  • Breastfeeding, body weight, height, length and wellbeing

Duration: KMC should be gradually increased and continued up to 20+ hours a day by mothers and other family members.

Discontinuation: Often this is desirable until the baby's gestation reaches term (40 weeks corrected gestational age) or the weight is 2500 gm. When the mother and baby are comfortable, KMC is continued for as long as possible, at the institution & then at home. When baby starts wriggling to show that she/he is uncomfortable, pulls limbs out, cries and fusses every time the mother tries to put baby back to skin-to-skin care, KMC can be discontinued.
