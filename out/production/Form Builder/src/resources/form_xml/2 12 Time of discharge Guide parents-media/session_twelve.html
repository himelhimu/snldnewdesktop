Prepare parents for home care by helping them understand key messages:

  • Continue exclusive breastfeeding for 6 completed months and recognize signs of successful breastfeeding:
    • Feeds every 2-3 hours or 8-12 times per day
    • Sleeps well between feedings
    • Passes urine at least 6-8 times a day from around 6th days of age
  • Recognize and manage common breast problems:
    • Flat/short/ inverted nipple
    • Cracked nipple
    • Breast engorgement
    • Mastitis, Breast abscess
  • Wash hands before touching the baby and put nothing on the cord
  • Immunize as per EPI schedule
  • Recognize Danger Signs including severe jaundice. Understand the need to seek medical care urgently for these signs. Understand where this care would be obtained
  • Stable premature babies or babies with more than 2000 gm birth weight should receive Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC) after birth in the hospital and should be continued at home after discharge till the baby reaches corrected full term(40 weeks) or weighs 2500 gm which ever comes first.

These messages can be reinforced during Pre discharge Counseling. Parents should demonstrate their knowledge of these key messages.

Provide information on follow up care; when and where it will be provided.

In case of first PNC visit:

  • If birth at health facility- provide PNC services before discharge or within 24 hours after birth, whichever comes first
  • If birth at home- first PNC contact should be made within 24 hours of birth

Subsequent PNC on third day, between 7-14 days after birth and six weeks after birth:

Programmatically, PNC home visit by community health worker should be made within 48 hours, and other PNC visits should be provided on day 7, and day 42 at the community clinic/UH&FWC/any other facility.
