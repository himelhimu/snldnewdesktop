Information and support from a health care provider will increase a mother’s confidence and chance of successful early and exclusive breastfeeding.

A baby, who is ready to breastfeed looks around, opens the mouth, with the head straight back with tongue down and forward and licks. Place the baby in a position to breastfed when these signs appear.

Mother can be breastfeed at her comfortable position (either sitting or lying down) and shouldn’t bend towards the baby. Baby’s body should be straight; head and shoulder must be in one line, baby should be close to the mother and babies whole bobdy should be supported (espicially buttock) by mother’s hand.

Both good positioning and attachment is essential for successful breastfeeding. During breastfeeding mother should be comfortable.

Signs of good position

  • The mother should place the baby with the head and body in a straight line
  • The face being opposite the nipple, chin touching the breast and the neck not flexed
  • The whole body should be supported

Signs of good attachment

  • Mouth is open wide
  • Lower lip is turned outward
  • Chin is touching the breast
  • More areola is inside the baby’s mouth

Poor attachment occurs when the baby pulls at the nipple and only the nipple is in the mouth. Poor attachment may cause the baby to take in more air and have abdominal distention and pain. Mothers may experience nipple pain with poor attachment.

Baby should be fed on demand, usually every 2-3 hours (8-12 times) a day including night time feeds. Baby getting enough milk is satisfied between feeds, sleeps well, passes urine at least 6-8 times per 24 hours, and gains average weight 20-40 gm per day.
