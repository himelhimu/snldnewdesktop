  • Dry the baby thoroughly at birth
    Drying helps keep the baby warm and stimulate breathing. A newly born wet baby can become cold even in a warm room. Dry the head, body, arms, legs and back by gently wiping with a pre-warmed clean cloth. Remove the wet cloth.
  • If there is meconium in the amniotic fluid, clear the airway before drying
    If the baby has passed stool before birth, there is meconium in the amniotic fluid. Clear the airway before drying when there is meconium in the amniotic fluid. Meconium inhaled into the lungs can cause breathing problems. Suck the mouth first and then nose immediately after delivery. Dry the baby thoroughly after clearing the airway.
