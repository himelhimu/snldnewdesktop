According to WHO and national recommendation, application of 7.1% Chlorhexidine on recently cut umbilical cord is a part of the essential newborn care package.


Recently cut umbilical cord is a common portal of entry for invasive bacteria. Bacteria rapidly colonize the moist cord stump and have direct access to the blood stream through umbilical vessels even without causing local infections.


Application of 7.1% Chlorhexidine is a proven intervention to reduce newborn morbidity and mortality. Single application will be followed by dry cord care. No other antiseptic or other substance should be used after application of 7.1% Chlorhexidine.


Application of 7.1% Chlorhexidine on the newborn umbilical cord is applicable for all newborn irrespective of the place of delivery.

What is Chlorhexidine?

  • Chlorhexidine is a safe antiseptic for newborn
  • Strength and formulation- 7.1% Chlorhexidine Digluconate solution
  • 10 ml bottle of 7.1% Chlorhexidine for single use

How to apply Chlorhexidine?

Provider/caregiver must wash hands before applying 7.1% Chlorhexidine

7.1% Chlorhexidine should be applied by pressing the dropper bottle over umbilical stump, body and base so that the umbilical cord is fully soaked

The cord should be kept clean and dry (Dry cord care).


DONâ€™TS: Do not put bandage or cover the cord or apply anything to the cord including herbs, mustard oil, hot compression, animal dung, shindur (vermilion), boric powder, antibiotic and antiseptics. If blood oozes from the cord, apply a new tie tightly between the skin and first tie.

Who will apply?

In case of facility delivery, the service provider should apply 7.1% Chlorhexidine on the umbilical stump, document it and inform the mother and family members; in case of home delivery, service provider or the family members can apply it.

When to apply?

Immediately after cutting the cord.

If not applied immediately, apply within 48 hours of birth.
