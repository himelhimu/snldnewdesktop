After completing a “quick check”, immediately assess the gestational age of any woman who has one of the 4 conditions leading to preterm birth. ACS has proven benefits from 24-34 weeks. When exact pregnancy dating is unavailable it is recommended to give ACS to women with estimated gestational ages between 24-34 weeks regardless of other complications of pregnancy. This expanded range is recommended because ACS has dramatic potential benefits with few to no risks.

Some methods of estimating gestational age are more accurate than others. For example, Ultrasounds done earlier in pregnancy are more accurate than ultrasounds done later in pregnancy (+/- 1 week versus +/- 2 weeks). Fundal heights are only accurate +/- 3 weeks. Always use more than one method to determine gestational age.

Tool to determine gestational age:

  • Last menstrual period (LMP)
  • Fundal height
  • Review or perform ultrasonogram (USG) if available

Reviewing LMP

Although women may not be aware of the date of their last menstrual period, they can often remember significant events that were happening at the same time. Use clues such as the following to help associate LMP with a datable event:

  • Holidays or harvesting time
  • Important events, local or regional market days
  • Memorable weather events- flood, storm
  • Important personal dates: birthdays, anniversaries, travel

Determining GA from LMP

To determine GA from LMP by using the Calendar method - by using the following formula we can calculate the estimated due date:

  • (First day of LMP + 9 months) + (7 days)

Then, to calculate the GA, count how many weeks remain between today’s date and the EDD, then subtract that number from 40.

Symphysio-Fundal Height in cm

Gestational age roughly matches the fundal height in cm from 20 weeks onwards. If fundal height differs from LMP by >3 weeks, an ultrasound is recommended to check for multiple gestations and to verify dating.

Fundal Height in wks of gestation

  • 12 wk- Just palpable above symphysis pubis
  • 16 wk- Midway between symphysis pubis and umbilicus
  • 20 wk- lower border of umbilicus
  • 24 wk- upper border of umbilicus
  • 28 wk- Junction of lower third and upper two third of distance between umbilicus and tip of xiphoid process
  • 32 wk- Junction of lower two third and upper third of distance between umbilicus and tip of xiphoid process.
  • 36 wk- At the level of tip of xiphoid process.
  • 34 & 40 wk- Midway between 32 & 36 wks

How to measure Fundal height

Have the woman lie flat on her back. Place one end of measuring tape on the symphysis pubis and the other at the top edge of the fundus, feeling with the edge of your hand.
