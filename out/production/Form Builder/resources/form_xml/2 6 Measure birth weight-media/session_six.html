All babies should be weighed within 90 minutes of birth. Birth weight classifies and identifies babies who may need special care, provides a baseline for monitoring growth and necessary for calculating doses of fluids and drugs.

Use only baby scales for weighing babies.

  • Wash hands
  • Wash the baby scale with alcohol solution or 0.5% chlorine solution
  • Put the clean cloth on the scale
  • Turn the scale to zero
  • Undress the baby
  • Place the baby on the scale
  • Press hold button, when baby is settled
  • Record the weight
  • Dress the baby properly

Always document birth weight.

Babies with low birth weights may require special care to prevent low body temperature. Some small babies may also need alternative feeding methods and more frequent assessment to discover problems and Danger Signs.

  • < 2500 gm babies require special care for feeding and appropriate thermal care to prevent loss of body temperature
  • Between 1800 to 2000 gm babies should receive Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC) to ensure feeding, thermal care and infection prevention. KMC should be initiated in the hospital
  • From 1200 gm to less than 1800 gm, babies should receive KMC after initial stabilization
  • < 1200 gm babies should receive advanced care (e.g. gavage feedings or intravenous fluids) and when the baby becomes stable, Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC) should be initiated
