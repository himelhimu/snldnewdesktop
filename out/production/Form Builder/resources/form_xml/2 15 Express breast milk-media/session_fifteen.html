Mothers may express milk from their breasts to feed their babies who are unable to feed from the breast. Also some mothers may express milk to relieve engorgement. Expression of milk may also facilitate latching on to the nipple when a baby is not able to suck effectively.

Milk should be expressed at approximately the same frequency of breastfeeding. Breast milk may be produced in small amounts initially, but production typically increases after 2-3 days.

Collect breast milk in a clean container with a lid if it is to be stored.

Steps of expression of breast milk:

  • Prior to expressing milk, mothers should wash their hands with soap and water and clean their breasts with water, but not soap.
  • Place a clean container below the breast to collect milk.
  • Massage the breast gently towards the nipple.
  • Place your thumb and index finger opposite each other just outside the areola.
  • Now press back towards the chest, then gently squeeze to express milk.
  • Repeat step 4 at different segments around the areola.
  • Express breast until milk drips, then express the other breast.
  • Alternate between breasts 5-6 times (20-30minutes).
  • Massage breasts and apply warm compressions prior to or during expression to improve milk flow.

Express milk at the times when a baby would normally feed (every 2-3 hours and at least 8-12 times during a 24 hour period).

Breast milk can be kept in a cool place for up to 6 hours or up to 24 hours if refrigerated and can be stored for up to 3 months in freezer at -200C/ -40F. Use freshly expressed milk whenever possible.
