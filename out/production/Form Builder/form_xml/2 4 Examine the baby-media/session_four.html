Newborn will be examined within 90 minutes of birth to assess the wellbeing and detect any anomaly. The provider should discuss the newbornâ€™s progress with the mother and finds out if there is any concern about the baby.

The history and examination include the following components:

  • Check breathing every 15 minutes, noting especially chest in-drawing, grunting and a slow or rapid rate of breathing after birth
  • Check the temperature: Feel the hand, feet and abdomen for warmth and if the baby feels cold to touch
  • Check feeding and observe breast feeding
  • Observe the cord stump
  • Check skin color
  • Observe activity and body movement
  • Measure body weight
  • Check for heart murmurs, jaundice, oral thrush
  • Inspect the following body areas for abnormalities in a systemic way: head, face, mouth and palate, chest, abdomen, genitalia, anus, limbs, back and skin
  • Ask for frequency of passing urine and meconium

Criteria of a Normal Newborn

  • Respiratory rate: 30-59 breaths/min
  • Heart rate: 100-160 beats/min
  • Temperature: 36.50-37.50C/97.50-99.50F (axillary, recorded over 3 minutes)
  • Birth weight: 2500 gm to 4000 gm
  • Color: Pink but slight peripheral cyanosis for a few hours after birth
  • Movement: Spontaneous, equal, arms and legs are flexed
  • Cord stump: No bleeding or drainage
  • Feeding: Able to breastfeed soon after birth
  • No apparent congenital malformation
  • No birth trauma
  • Passes meconium within 24 hours of birth
  • Passes urine within 48 hours of birth

Every provider should

  • Record the findings of examination
  • Keep baby warm during examination
  • If there is any abnormality, manage accordingly
  • Share examination findings to parents
