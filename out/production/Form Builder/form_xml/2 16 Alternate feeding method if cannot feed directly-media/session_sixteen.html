Some small babies, sick babies, or those with an abnormality such as cleft lip and palate, may be able to swallow but cannot suck effectively or they may suck effectively for a brief period but get tired before an adequate volume has been taken. These babies may benefit from being fed expressed milk dripped from the breast directly into the mouth, or by being fed with a cup.

For cup feeding, take a measured amount of milk in a clean cup. Baby should be awake and held semi-upright in caregiver’s lap. Place a small piece of cloth on baby’s chest to catch the spillage and touch the edge of the cup to the outer side of lower lip.

Start with 5-10 ml (2-5ml/kg) per feeding and gradually increase the amount. Begin with a total fluid intake for the day of birth at 60ml/kg and increase 20ml/kg each day until 150ml/kg per day is reached. Feeding mother’s milk promotes growth, helps prevent infection, and decreases mortality.

The baby is ready to feed when she is awake, looking around, with mouth open or licking. When expressing milk directly in to baby’s mouth, first let the baby smell and lick the nipple. Begin by dripping a small amount of milk into the baby’s mouth. Let the baby swallow before dripping more milk. When using a cup, allow the baby to lick the milk directly rather than pouring milk into the mouth. Tip/tilt the cup slightly so that the milk reaches the baby’s lip. Now baby would be able to sip herself/himself. Try avoiding pouring milk into baby’s mouth.

Consider advanced care (e.g., nasogastric feeding or intravenous fluids) if a baby is unable to swallow or cannot take adequate amounts.
